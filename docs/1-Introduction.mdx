## Why this exists

A design system is not a component library. It is a **language and a contract** — a shared way of describing what something *means* before describing what it *looks like*. Cards, colours, and easing curves are the alphabet; the contract is what makes them combine into legible product.

This system carries three contracts:

1. **Tokens describe role, not appearance.** `surface-authority` is the colour of legal weight. Today it's deep navy; tomorrow it could shift, and every component would follow.
2. **Foundations come before components.** You cannot build a Card before deciding what *raised* means. Read the Foundations section first.
3. **Accessibility is a default, not a feature.** Every colour pair is contrast-checked. Every transition respects `prefers-reduced-motion`. Every interactive element has a visible focus state.
